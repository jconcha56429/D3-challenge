Correlations Discovered Between several factors from the US Census Bureau, 2014 ACS 1-year estimates

The default graph displays the correlation between the mean of income and mean of healthcare between states. I initially hypothesized that these factors would be positively correlated. However after constructing the graph for it, it almost looks as if there's a negative correlation, albeit a small one. This may be the case because people with higher incomes can afford a more healthy lifestyle. In addition they may also be more prepared for health related issues because of their large income.

Another correlation worth noting would be between smoking and obesity. From a visual standpoint it seems that there's a positive correlation between the two. This may be because obese individuals may have low self-control and it turn are more likely to turn to unhealthy habits.

But, enough from me! Give my interactive project a try and see what correlations lie within this 3x3 factor graph.
